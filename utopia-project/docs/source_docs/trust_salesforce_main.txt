## Core Value Proposition

Salesforce emphasizes trust as their "#1 value," stating they "earn the trust of our customers, employees, and extended family through transparency, security, compliance, privacy, and performance."

## Trust Components

The platform organizes trust information across four main pillars:

- Status: Real-time service availability and performance transparency
- Security: Built-in security throughout operations
- Compliance: Cloud-engineered compliance frameworks
- Availability: Shared responsibility between Salesforce and customers

## Active Security Advisories

Experience Cloud Guest User Configurations: Salesforce reports monitoring threats targeting misconfigured public-facing sites, noting "we have not identified any vulnerability inherent to the Salesforce platform."

Phishing Campaign Alert: Active warnings about fraudulent emails impersonating Salesforce promoting Remote Monitoring & Management software installation.

Social Engineering Threats: Ongoing monitoring of credential theft campaigns targeting third-party identity providers, with emphasis that issues "do not stem from a vulnerability inherent to Salesforce."

Third-Party App Incident: Drift app (Salesloft) experienced compromise; Salesforce has re-enabled Salesloft integrations except Drift, pending further notice.

## Compliance Notice

Salesforce prohibits software provision to Russia under U.S., EU, and UK sanctions regulations without legal authorization.
